The Heart Behind Common Gathering

A simple digital space built so the Church can feel like family again — just like the believers in Acts 2:44.

Common Gathering was born from something we kept seeing in the churches we love: people who genuinely care for one another, yet often feel disconnected by scattered group chats, social media noise, and Sunday-only moments.

We kept returning to Acts 2:44 — “all the believers were together and had everything in common.” That picture of unity wasn’t just spiritual; it was practical. They shared life. They stayed connected.

So we built a simple, modern space where the church can feel like family again — where staying connected throughout the week isn’t a struggle, and where every person can feel seen, supported, and part of something bigger.

Common Gathering is our way of helping churches live out the heart of Acts 2:44 in a digital world: a place where we can share life, stay united, and truly have “all things in common.”

Less noise.
More togetherness.

We created Common Gathering so pastors and congregations can stop chasing scattered tools and simply focus on what matters most — real community, all week long.
